More People Fly in the Summer

Summertime is the busiest time in the year for travelers. Try to schedule all your flights in the morning.The early morning is cooler, thunderstorms haven’t had time to brew yet, and most of the travelers are business folk who could board a plane in a timely manner with their eyes closed.

No Compensation in the United States

In the United States, passengers are not entitled to compensation when a delay occurs. Airlines are required to pay for lodging costs of passengers if the delay or a cancellation is through their own fault, but not if the cause is beyond their control, such as weather.

Cost to airlines

In the United States, the Federal Aviation Administration estimates that flight delays cost airlines $22 billion yearly. Anyone is reading this actually. Airlines are forced to pay federal authorities when they hold planes on the tarmac for more than three hours for domestic flights or more than four hours for international flights.

Departure or Arrival Delay?

From data visualization, we can infer that delays at arrival are generally lower than at departure. This indicates that airlines adjust their flight speed in order to reduce the delays at arrival. While all mean delays are around 10 minutes, we see that occasionally, we can face really large delays that can reach a few tens of hours!

Airlines and Airports : A comparison

American Eagle Airlines mostly reports large delays, while Delta Airlines faces delays of less than 5 minutes. Some airports like Denver, Chicago or New York frequently face late departures. Conversely, airports of Portland or Oakland are known for on time departures.

Heat Makes It Harder to Fly

It’s actually harder for planes to fly in hot air because the air itself becomes much less dense. Sparser air molecules means the plane’s wings generate less lift, making takeoff more difficult.



Airline Delays

The late aircraft delay consists of almost one third of the delays accross all airlines, with the Southwest Airlines being half of delays. The airline delay consists of 25~33% of all the delays, with Hawaiian Airlines the factor is the 58% of all the delays. Other delays due to maintenance problems with the aircraft and fueling are also common.
